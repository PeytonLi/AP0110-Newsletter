# California Is Building Its Own AI Cloud

The University of California is launching CalCompute — a full-stack AI Cloud Platform for California government agencies, universities, and public institutions. Built to research standards, operated for the public good.

Today, we're sharing what we've been building and how you can get involved.

## What Is CalCompute?

CalCompute is public compute infrastructure for California's public sector: the same GPU compute, cloud storage, and AI inference capabilities as commercial cloud — operated as shared public infrastructure, without vendor lock-in.

The platform is hosted by 10 world-class UC research universities, home to Turing Award winners, Nobel laureates, and the researchers who are defining modern AI.

**For government agencies and public institutions**, CalCompute provides:

- **California Data Residency** — Your data stays in California, meeting HIPAA, FERPA, CJIS, and state compliance requirements by design.
- **Predictable Public Pricing** — Flat, allocation-based costs built for government budget cycles. No variable billing, no surprise charges, no per-token pricing.
- **No Vendor Lock-In** — Open standards throughout. Your data and tools work with any system — no proprietary formats, no exit fees.

## Research Access: Free Allocations at Launch

UC-affiliated researchers and California academic institutions will receive free compute allocations at launch. The research platform includes:

- **GPU Compute** — Distributed training runs, batch jobs, and interactive Jupyter sessions. CUDA and ROCm environments pre-configured. Allocations available for short exploratory experiments and multi-week training jobs.
- **Research Storage** — Petabyte-scale object storage with an S3-compatible API, built for large datasets, model checkpoints, and training artifacts. High-throughput access, no egress fees.
- **AI Inference APIs** — Serve and query open-weights models through an OpenAI-compatible REST API. Deploy custom inference endpoints at scale. Allocation-based access — no per-token pricing, no usage caps that interrupt long experiments.

## Powered by the UC System

When the University of California builds infrastructure, it's built to research standards — with the data controls, open standards, and procurement model that government workloads require.

> "Commercial clouds are built for private enterprise. CalCompute is built for public institutions."

The coalition spans all 10 UC campuses, bringing together the technical expertise and institutional trust needed to operate critical public infrastructure at scale.
